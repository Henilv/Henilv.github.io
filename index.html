Adversarial Machine Learning — Privacy & Security

My research work focused on evaluating the implicit algorithmic biases in ML systems, focusion on training‑time & inference‑time adversarial threats. This includes data poisoning, adversarial perturbations, membership inference attacks, and privacy‑leakage pathways. My work focuses on understanding how inherent flaws in ML pipelines create measurable security weaknesses.

Privacy-Preserving ML (GDPR, Unlearning, Federated Learning)

The research is encouraged by belief of technology through regulation and studies how mathematical privacy guarantees in ML systems using differential privacy, federated learning, PATE frameworks, and privacy‑preserving aggregation ensure privacy bounds. One emerging technique I explore is Machine Unlearning—where a model “forgets” specific data points it had previously learned, ensuring compliance with GDPR’s one of the attribute right-to-be-forgotten. My work focuses on building privacy‑aligned, resilient ML models across distributed and sensitive environments and loosely couples with fairness in algorithmic decision making
